Trouble aka Sin

the first Masked version

It all started with a random German woman, who had some fun hobbies—and one of them was talking to people she met online through TikTok. She did that for a few years, casually collecting stories, laughs, and a few "what just happened" moments along the way.

Until one day she thought, "Hmm… why not try on a mask to put a bit more focus on my eyes?" Silly, right?!

Except… it wasn't.

Because somehow, that one tiny, random decision turned into something way bigger than expected. People stayed longer. They listened closer. They remembered her. Not just as "that random woman online," but as something a little more mysterious, a little more captivating… a little more her.

And what started as a joke—just a mask, just a small change—slowly became part of the story she didn't even realize she was writing.

A story about finding connection in the most unexpected places. About how something as simple as covering your face could somehow make you feel more seen.

Funny how that works, isn't it?

It was never just about the mask.

People didn't turn away—they leaned in.

And suddenly, it wasn't just one person behind a screen anymore… it was a space filled with expression, connection, and this quiet understanding between strangers who didn't feel like strangers at all.

Funny how it grew from something so small into something that actually meant something.

The Foxy-Maid

Dokomi 2026

I know life gets weird sometimes.

We lose track of each other for a while. Things get messy, overwhelming… and suddenly everything feels like it's falling apart, like we've failed somehow. Those moments where all you want to do is just sit there and cry.

Honestly… the first day of DoKoMi this year was exactly that for me.

It felt like everything that could go wrong, did. One problem after another during my travels, to the point where I ended up missing the entire first day of the convention. And emotionally… it was just pure hell.

But even on that day—especially on that day—some of you reminded me of something really important.

That even on your worst days, you don't have to be alone.

You don't have to carry everything by yourself.

Because some of you checked up on me. You listened to me ramble, complain, and honestly just vent about everything going wrong… and you stayed.

And that's the thing about this community.

We show up for each other.

Yes, it's not perfect. There are darker sides, difficult moments—because that's just how people are sometimes. But even with that… look at how much care exists here.

Look at how much love, patience, and understanding we give one another.

And somehow, that always ends up mattering more.

The Fairy

Dokomi 2026

And now… just one more sprinkle of fairy dust.

Because somehow—some way—I actually did it.

I was a fairy.

Yes… that kind of fairy. "She was a fairy." The kind you don't fully believe in until you suddenly do. The kind that feels like a story someone tells you as a child, and you carry it quietly with you, hoping it might be real someday.

And for a moment… it was.

And you—my beloved weirdos, my chaos, my comfort—you saw me like that and didn't question it. You didn't laugh it away or brush it off. You leaned into it. You told me it suited me, like it was something that had always been there, just waiting to be seen.

And I think… that meant more to me than I can properly put into words.

Because maybe being a fairy was never about wings or outfits or a silly little TikTok sound.

Maybe it was about that feeling.

That soft kind of magic that exists between people who found each other by chance, but stayed on purpose. The kind that lingers in late-night conversations, in shared laughter, in the quiet understanding of "I see you" without needing to say it out loud.

A little lost, a little wild, a little stitched together from all the moments that almost broke us—but didn't.

Like something out of a story where no one really knows where they're going… but no one wants to leave, either.

So if you're still here—if you've been here—if you'll find your way here someday…

I hope you're ready.

For more magic. More chaos. More softness. More everything.

More stories that feel like they don't belong anywhere else but somehow belong exactly here with us. More laughter that comes out of nowhere and stays longer than expected. More moments where we don't have it all figured out, but we have each other—and that's enough.

Because this journey?

It was never meant to end here.

We're still growing. Still changing. Still becoming versions of ourselves we haven't even met yet.

And I don't know exactly what the future looks like… but I know this:

If there's fairy dust to be scattered, stories to be written, and a little bit of chaos to be shared—

I want it to be with you.

So here's to everything that's been, everything that is, and everything that's still waiting for us somewhere just out of reach…

like a story we're still in the middle of telling.
